History

Land Rover was founded in 1948, and it has been a leading manufacturer of off-road and luxury SUVs worldwide.

  • 1948: The Land Rover Series I debuted at the Amsterdam Motor Show. It was a rugged, all-terrain vehicle built for farming and military use.
  • 1958: The Series II introduced a wider body and more engine options.
  • 1971: The Series III became the most produced Land Rover, featuring improvements like a full synchromesh gearbox.
  • 1983: The Land Rover 90 and 110 (later renamed Defender) were introduced. The Defender became famous for its durability and was widely used in military and adventure expeditions.
  • 1994: Rover Group (including Land Rover) was acquired by BMW.
  • 2000: BMW sold Land Rover to Ford, which modernized the lineup.
  • 2008: Ford sold Land Rover (along with Jaguar) to Tata Motors, forming Jaguar Land Rover (JLR).
  • 2020: Land Rover launched a completely redesigned Defender, bringing modern styling with advanced off-road tech.
